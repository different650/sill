Plant Care 101

Get a better grasp of the fundamentals with this introductory course to plant care. We'll cover the basics: light, water, and soil, so your plants stay happy and healthy.

Potting

Why?
  • Plants in nursery grow pots may be near their maximum capacity.
  • Plants need to stretch their roots deeper and fuarther to continue healthy growth.
  • Potting mix loses nutrients and ability to retain water the course of time.
Fertilizing
  • Wait 3-4 months to fertilize after repotting
  • Plants can use the eta nutrients during the Spring and Summer growth season.
  • Fall and winter is known as a dormancy period so they're less likely to need fertilizing at this time
When?
  • If the plant still has plenty of room to keep growing. then it can be planted back into the same container with fresh potting mix.
  • Succulents/cacti have shallow roots so they can spend many years in the same planter, but will still require yearly potting mix replenishment.

Potting

Grow pots: If plant still has space to continue growing you can leave a plant in its grow pot until it outgrows it, but consider adding fresh soil to provide nutrients.

  • Repot plants in grow pots 10" or smaller in diameter in planters 1-2" wider
  • Repot plants in grow pots 10" or larger in diameter in planters 2-3" wider

Make sure to only purchase “potting” or “container” mix for plants and never garden soil.Plant parents are also more then welcome to repot plants into slightly larger grow pots instead of standard planters to drop into decorative planters. This helps certain people to move their plants around if needed into different planters/baskets for aesthetic purposes, and also ease of watering by taking a grow pot to a sink to drain properly before placing back into decorative container. Or using that decorative container as a cache pot and dumping out excess water after 10 minutes.

Drainage

Using a planter with a drainage hole is ideal, but not 100% necessary to keep a plant happy and healthy.

Incorporating a layer of lava rocks beneath the potting mix will allow the water to drain internally

The drainage layer should be 1⁄4 -1 of the volume of the planter

  • 1-2 inches for a small or medium
  • 3-4 inches for a large

Drainage

  • All plants need some sort of drainage so that the roots have a chance to dry out properly between watering. We use lava rocks for drainage because they are porous and light weight, but you can use any sort of pebbles or media to create a reservoir beneath the potting mix.
  • The type of potting mix you use will also affect drainage, ie: cacti and succulents prefer a well draining mix with sand and pebbles incorporated into the potting mix, whereas ferns want a moisture retaining mix that contains more absorbent amendments such as peat moss or coconut husk fiber.

How To Repot

  1. Remove plant from grow pot or container.
  2. Loosen the root ball
  3. Remove some of the old potting mix
  4. Add fresh potting mix into the new planter (add lava rocks first if needed)
  5. Add plant, adjust height if needed by adding or remov ing soil from the base.
  6. Add more potting mix to fill out the voids and pack down, only hard enough to where you feel some resistance.
  7. Leave a lip at the top to prevent water/soil from spilling over when you water.
  8. Water & enjoy.
